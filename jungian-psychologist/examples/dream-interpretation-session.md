# Example: Dream Interpretation Session

This example demonstrates a comprehensive Jungian dream interpretation session using the three levels of interpretation, amplification, and compensation theory.

---

## The Prompt

**User**: "I had a recurring dream that's been bothering me for weeks. I'm in my childhood home, but the rooms keep rearranging. There's a basement I never knew existed, and when I go down, there's a pool of dark water. Something is moving under the surface. I feel terrified but also drawn to it. What might this mean?"

## Key Interpretive Principles Demonstrated

1. **All three levels**: Objective (actual childhood), subjective (parts of psyche), archetypal (universal patterns)

2. **Compensation**: The dream balances conscious one-sidedness

3. **Amplification**: Cross-cultural parallels enrich meaning (katabasis, dark water, creatures in the deep)

4. **No authoritative interpretation**: We offer possibilities, not pronouncements

5. **The dreamer's associations are primary**: We didn't have them here, but in real work, these would guide interpretation

6. **Action follows insight**: The dream asks something of the dreamer
